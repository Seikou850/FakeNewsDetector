WHAT IS A FAKE NEWS?

The Fake News or false news, are news, stories, articles, etc. misleading, created to deliberately misinform or mislead the reader. Normally, fake news is often used to influence user opinions, set the political agenda or generate disinformation, among other purposes. Usually they are impact news, susceptible to being viralized before being contrasted, in this way they manage to spread quickly, being difficult to stop once identified. Despite the fact that the public is currently aware of this type of news, its evolution does not stop and it continues to expand in the form of data, images and even videos.


WHAT TYPES OF FAKE NEWS ARE THERE?

Fake news primarily serves to manipulate the public for certain political or commercial purposes through deliberately misrepresented news. However, fake news can have other purposes as well. For example, some tabloid headlines look to clickbait, which aims to get the link on the website to get a lot of clicks and thus more ad revenue. Phishing also makes use of false information and takes advantage of the good faith of Internet users. They are often asked to enter their personal information in apparently reliable forms, which can lead to identity Another phenomenon is chain mail hoaxes, threatening recipients with fatal consequences if they are not forwarded.
Next, we present four types of fairly frequent fake news:
⚫Deliberate misinformation: news invented and distributed with specific interests. Normally, these news are directed to groups especially susceptible to this type of information, who qualify the content as credible and share it on their social networks without verifying it.
⚫Bogus Headlines: Headlines featuring bogus facts to gain attention have long been a popular stylistic method among the non-serious media. Often, when reading the article, it is discovered that the headline is deliberately misleading and does not correspond to the content of the news. Fake headlines are also known as clicbait headlines.
⚫Viral Posts: On social media, new news is posted every second. Therefore, users do not take their time to verify the authenticity of each publication. Since large platforms are based on how many times a post has been shared, "likes" and followers, popular posts are shown more frequently even when they are not true, as in the case of fake news.
⚫Satire: Satirical news talks about current controversies and mixes them with made-up and often absurd events. It is a stylistic device to draw attention to social problems or denounce political misconduct. However, there is a danger that the humorous component is not recognized and is confused with reality.
